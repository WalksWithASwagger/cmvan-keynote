## Movement 1: The Cassette Tape (The Hook)
*Visuals: Raw, static, VHS glitch aesthetics.*
- **The Story:** Introduce Matt Lock. He sat on cassette tapes of his music for 27 years. He couldn't finish it. 
- **The Turn:** AI didn't replace his creativity. It gave him the last 15% he needed to finally release *R3ALMS* in November 2025. It wasn't replacement; it was liberation.
- **The Thesis:** Every cultural shift starts the same way: someone picks up a cheap tool and uses it wrong.

## Movement 2: The Mother of Punk (The Context)
*Visuals: Dada cutouts flowing into Xeroxed zines (Sniffin' Glue).*
- **The Lineage:** 
  - 1916: Dada poets cut up newspapers (sampling before vinyl).
  - Burroughs does it with scissors.
  - 1976: A bank clerk xeroxes 60 copies of *Sniffin' Glue* on a defense contractor's machine. Punk had a press.
  - Kingston: The selector picks records at the sound system dance because the skill wasn't making the sound—it was knowing which one the room needed. That took a boat to the Bronx and became hip-hop.
- **The Pattern:** Corporations build the tools. Weirdos figure out what they're actually for.

## Movement 3: Our Xerox Machine (The Conflict)
*Visuals: Flickr logo fading into a massive AI data center, hard contrast.*
- **The Reality:** AI is just the next copy shop. Built by the biggest corporations on earth. 
- **The Theft:** Trained on stolen work. "Roughly 1,800 of my own photos, scraped from Flickr without my permission or consent. Nobody asked. They just took it."
- **The Tension:** "And here’s the part I can’t look away from: I’m also more creative, more productive, and more powerful than I’ve ever been in my entire life."
- **The Punk Condition:** Both facts are true. Both hands full. Critique in one hand, capability in the other. That's not contradiction. That's complexity. If the critical thinkers opt out, the people who don't see the problem will write the rules.

## Movement 4: The Selector's Era (The Pivot)
*Visuals: A DJ mixing board transitioning into a text prompt interface.*
- **The Paradigm Shift:** In 2005, anyone with a blog was a tastemaker because generation was expensive and curation was free. Now, AI generates everything instantly. 
- **The New Scarcity:** Taste is the new scarcity. The bottleneck isn't making, it's choosing. 
- **The Call:** We all have to become selectors. 

## Movement 5: The Tactical Toolkit (The Practice)
*Visuals: High-speed screencast of prompt engineering, heavily stylized.*
- **Name What You See:** Stop saying "bias." Say theft. Say misogyny. Say racism. AI knows what's frequent, not what's fair. The mirror reflects, but doesn't correct. You do.
- **The Three Documents Hack:** How to externalize your weird, specific, un-googleable creative instincts. 
  1. Personal AI Policy
  2. Writing Style Guide
  3. Worldview Document
- **The Result:** The machine stops giving you average, and starts amplifying what makes you *you*.

## Movement 6: The Release Day Challenge (The Call to Action)
*Visuals: Bold, gritty typography: MAY 29.*
- **The Setup:** Vancouver is the home of Adbusters and Greenpeace. We turn corporate infrastructure into counterculture.
- **The Challenge:** Use the month of May to externalize your taste. Feed it to the machine.
- **The Delivery:** Ship something only you could've made by Release Day, May 29th. A zine, a short film, a photo series. Not polished. Not precious. Punk never was.
- **The Final Drop:** "Let's use these tools to f*** it up. You coming?"
