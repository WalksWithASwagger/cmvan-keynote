# Story Bank: Punk Rock AI

Detailed narratives of the case studies and creator stories referenced in the keynote.

## 1. Matt Lock (Matlock) — The 27-Year Cassette
- **The Setup:** A brilliant beatmaker and musician. He spent decades making music but could never get past the friction of finalizing, mastering, and the visual distribution components. He sat on cassette tapes for 27 years.
- **The Turn:** AI didn't write his music. It provided the specialized support structure—the final 15%—that allowed him to finally ship it.
- **The Result:** He released the *R3ALMS* album in November 2025. 
- **Key Quote:** "AI more for the enhancement of creativity versus the replacement of creativity."

## 2. Luke Minaker — The Monster Builder
- **The Setup:** Luke has spent 30 years building physical monsters and props for film. The Hollywood physical effect lifecycle meant his work was 80% physical drudgery and administrative scaling, leaving only 20% for raw creative joy.
- **The Turn:** Utilizing generative conceptualizing and AI workflows flipped his required labor ratios.
- **Key Quote:** "I don't care about the money. I am so excited that I get to do art again." This is liberation, not replacement.

## 3. Belgium — The Actress
- **The Setup:** An actress whose career was completely derailed. She was bedridden for 18 months due to severe illness, entirely separated from the physical capacity to act or film.
- **The Turn:** Text-to-Video and AI film allowed her to return to directing, acting (via avatars/generation), and storytelling without leaving her bed.
- **The Lesson:** When the ethics debates get overly abstract or focused on protecting legacy monopolies, we often forget the transformative power of *accessibility*.
- **Key Quote:** "AI film is my hope to be able to continue to exist and to tell something."

## 4. Kevin Friel — The Conductor
- **The Setup:** 25-year Hollywood VFX veteran who has seen every digital tool transition (from practical to CGI to Generative).
- **The Metaphor:** The Conductor. The generative tools do not replace the conductor; they simply give the conductor a much larger orchestra. 
- **The Method:** "Get it 85% there with AI, then use craft to finish."
- **Key Quote:** "There's something very punk rock about taking a tool that’s staring you in the face and using it to tell the stories that matter."

## 5. Vicki — The Visual Artist
- **The Setup:** An analog artist who struggled with writing and articulating her visual style into text. "I'm a visual person, not a writer."
- **The Turn:** Instead of struggling to prompt, she reverse-engineered it. She uploaded 20 of her previous photos into Claude/ChatGPT and asked the AI to analyze the patterns and describe her style.
- **The Lesson:** She realized her "intuition" wasn't magic—it was deep pattern recognition developed over years of practice. By using the AI to hold up a mirror, she could finally articulate her taste. Unexamined expertise can't be taught, but articulated taste scales.

## 6. The 1,800 Stolen Photos (Kris's Personal Story)
- **The Setup:** The core personal friction validating the "Punk Rock" stance. AI companies scraped datasets indiscriminately. 
- **The Reality:** Around 1,800 of Kris's personal photos on Flickr and under Creative Commons were absorbed by these models without permission or payment.
- **The Stance:** "I find my relationship with AI completely non-consensual."
- **The Pivot:** Instead of opting out, he uses the tools built on his stolen work to become more powerful than ever. Taking the master's tools to dismantle the master's house.
