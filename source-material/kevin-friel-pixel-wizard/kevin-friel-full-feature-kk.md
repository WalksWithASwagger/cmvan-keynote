# Mr. Pixel Wizard: Kevin Friel, Hollywood VFX, and the Punk Rock Heart of Vancouver's AI Film Club

**By Kris Krüg**

Kevin Friel will look you dead in the eye and tell you he took a project that used to sit in a six-figure budget column and shipped it for two hundred bucks in two and a half days—same quality bar he held at the big houses, same eye for comp and motion, same refusal to let the work look cheap.

He's not trying to dunk on the old world. He's trying to hand the new one to anyone willing to show up and learn.

That's Kevin: twenty-five years deep in the machine—DNEG, BRON, MPC, the names that show up on the back of your ticket stub—and now co-leading the **BC + AI Film Club** with **Luke Minaker** while **Brian Hockenstein** and a bench of volunteers keep the room warm. He's taught **1,500+** people ComfyUI through **Curious Refuge**. He runs WhatsApp threads where people debug nodes at midnight and still show up smiling at the **Multimodal Media Lab** for the monthly screening.

We call him **Mr. Pixel Wizard**. Sometimes **Prompt Goblin**. Affiliation line for the record: **Nera Studios** and **AI Film Club co-lead**—if that shifts, we update; the through-line doesn't.

## The number that stops polite conversation

Here's the line from our own quotes doc that I keep coming back to, because it's not a metaphor:

> "I reduced video production from $100,000 budgets to $200 in 2.5 days. Same quality. Real project."

Read that twice. Same person who used to price shots in the language of render farms and roto contracts is now pricing them like a indie band pays for van gas.

When **Luke** held up a **$100** AI-assisted spot next to what would have been a **$200K–400K** traditional pipeline in the room, people didn't nod politely—they leaned in. That's the Film Club effect: receipts, not vibes.

## Hollywood wasn't a fantasy camp—it was a grind

Kevin didn't stumble out of a YouTube tutorial into "filmmaker" as a hobby label. He earned the heavy version of the job: years inside facilities where real-time generative work meant **$25K** rigs and teams of specialists, where "no" came from producers, legal, and physics in about that order.

So when he talks about access, he's not recycling a Medium post. He's describing a life spent on the wrong side of the gate:

> "I spent 25 years learning the Hollywood way. Now I teach people to skip all that and just make things."

That's the generosity that sets the tone for our whole film subgroup. If Kevin shares, everyone shares. **Daniela Gamarra**, **Kris** (me), the lurkers who finally post a broken workflow—**1,174+** messages across **127+** participants in the Film Club WhatsApp since we spun it up in **June 2025**—it compounds because the co-leads model *no gatekeeping* as culture, not policy.

> "I don't gatekeep. Twenty-five years of VFX knowledge, and I'll tell you everything. That's the Film Club way."

## SIGGRAPH in a planetarium T-shirt

The night that still defines Kevin for a lot of people here was **Vancouver AI Meetup #20** (**August 2024**, **H.R. MacMillan Space Centre**). He didn't deliver a slide deck about "industry trends." He imported the part of **SIGGRAPH** that actually matters—the hallway energy—and put it on our stage.

**John Mutter** showed a fake home-shopping spot: **Stable Diffusion** stills, **Google VO3** video, human-written script ("the old way," as John said), **Nuke** comp. Four weeks of experiments that would have been a team-months problem a few seasons ago.

**Ahmed** demoed **Creative**—no-code game worlds, physics, weather, destruction, "vibe coding" a scene into existence—then handed the room a date for **October** launch like it was obvious.

Kevin's job that night wasn't to be the smartest person in the room. It was to be the **connector**: kill the stage lights when Ahmed asked, keep the pacing human, make sure the crowd understood why any of it mattered.

His closing beat was simple: the industry is in a copilot relationship with AI tools now—gaming, 3D, film, pick your silo—and Vancouver wasn't going to sit that out. We were starting a **film component** of the meetups, WhatsApp already live, first sessions soon.

That's how the Film Club lineage shows up in public: not a press release—a room full of people who saw the work.

## The stack he actually runs (and teaches)

Kevin's production brain isn't "pick one app and pray." It's chain thinking—**Midjourney** for frames, **Runway** for motion (including multi-reference and **Act 2** body performance), **ElevenLabs** for audio, **ComfyUI** to glue and customize, **Topaz** for polish.

He's been blunt that the social layer of AI tools gets noisy while the production layer still delivers genuine *wow*—and that the stack only matters if you ship.

> "ComfyUI isn't just a tool—it's a philosophy. Build your own workflow, don't wait for someone to sell you theirs."

> "The best workflow is the one you actually use. Fancy doesn't matter if it breaks your creative momentum."

That's the same instinct that made him dangerous in comp: he isn't romantic about nodes for their own sake. He's romantic about **getting the shot**.

## Punk rock AI (yes, on purpose)

If you've heard Kevin riff on **punk rock** and AI in the same breath, it isn't branding cosplay. It's a read on access:

> "There's something very punk rock about taking a tool that's staring you in the face and using it to tell the stories that matter."

> "Like punk rock emerging from Xerox copying machines, we're using the master's tools to tell stories of resistance."

> "We're not waiting for Hollywood's permission. We're building our own pipelines."

Which lands next to ethics without pretending they're separate topics. In our club principles—and in Kevin's public framing—**human creativity first** means you don't outsource your taste or your intent to a slider.

> "AI is the tool. You're the artist. Don't confuse which one has ideas."

Transparency shows up the same way: disclose when it matters; don't treat audiences like they're naive.

> "Disclose AI use when it matters. We're not precious about it, but we're honest."

And the festival floor version, which I love because it refuses moral panic while demanding craft:

> "Bay Raitt said it at the festival: 'Cinema has been lying since 1895. The question is: is it meaningful?' Same applies to AI."

That's the Film Club spine: **meaning** over **performance of purity**.

## The August 2025 conversation nobody clips enough

Everyone wants Kevin's render graph. Fewer people quote what happens when you put a mic in front of him about **how communities actually scale**.

In the **August 2025** member interview with **Gabriel Zaro**, Kevin described BC + AI less like a corporation and more like an organism learning its shape—**loose boundaries**, **community sentiment**, a **collective North Star** instead of a single strong-arm playbook. He talked about **pooling strengths across** centers—Kelowna, the Island, the interior—**constant dialogue**, **field trips**, dividing and conquering at **TIFF**'s **AI on the Lot** with **Patrick** while still doing the show-floor alliance work at **SIGGRAPH**.

He said the quiet part about success out loud: *it's conversations.* Opportunities ricochet because people answer texts and show up.

That's not a TED arc. That's infrastructure.

## Why this matters for BC (and why I'm writing it)

We keep proving the same thesis: the best creative AI work here isn't happening because a vendor flew in swag. It's happening because practitioners with receipts—**Kevin**, **Luke**, **John**, **Ahmed**, the people in our rows—trade workflows, argue ethics, and ship work that used to be "someone else's department."

Kevin is the bridge a lot of communities wish they had: fluent in studio-grade execution, allergic to mystique, willing to teach **1,500+** strangers on the internet and **127+** neighbors in WhatsApp with the same patience.

He also models something harder than tutorials: **distributed credit**. He'll hype Patrick's **SIGGRAPH** chair access, Kate Armstrong and Michael Tippett's recon, Neve's startup invite, Gabriel's community-intelligence frame—then go back to answering a beginner's mask question at **1am** because that's the job when you're serious about culture.

## Show up

If you make images, cut picture, write, design sound, or just want to stop fearing the tools—come to a **Film Club** night. Bring a broken project. Bring a hot take. Bring a friend who thinks AI art is "cheating" and let the room be kinder than the internet.

We don't need you to arrive polished. We need you to arrive **honest**.

> "The barrier to entry just disappeared. Now it's about ideas, not access to equipment."

Kevin already paid the tuition in decades. He's busy paying it forward in public.
