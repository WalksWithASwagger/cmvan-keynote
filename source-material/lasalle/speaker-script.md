## SLIDE 3: The Binary Trap
**[~2 min]**

"Every time AI comes up, you get pushed toward one of two camps.

**The Boosters** say: 'AI is the greatest thing ever. Stop whining and get on board. If you're not using it, you're already behind.'

**The Doomers** say: 'AI is stealing our work. It's destroying jobs. We should resist.'

**Both of these are lazy.**

They're lazy because they let you off the hook. If AI is just evil, you don't have to engage with it. If AI is just magic, you don't have to think critically about it.

Both positions avoid the harder work—which is holding complexity."

## SLIDE 6: The Five Fears
**[~5 min]**

"Let me name the fears. Because they're real.

**1. Stolen Work.** AI has been trained on the work of mankind. Without consent. No compensation. Your work might be in there. Mine definitely is.

**2. Race to the Bottom.** Clients can get 'good enough' for cheap. A logo that used to cost five thousand dollars? Someone generates one in 30 seconds. If you were competing on speed or volume, AI just undercut you.

**3. Junior Pipeline Collapse.** This is the one that keeps me up at night. Entry-level tasks that build senior creatives—media monitoring, basic research, first drafts—they're vanishing. How do we build seniors if juniors never get to do the reps? I teach AI for a living, and I don't have this solved. Anyone who tells you they do is lying.

**4. Cognitive Dependency.** What happens when we outsource thinking? When we stop drafting and only edit? When we lose the muscle because we never use it?

**5. Commodification.** If everyone can generate, what's left? If a client can make their own logo in 30 seconds, why do they need you?

I'm not dismissing these. I'm naming them. They're in my left hand too."

## SLIDE 11: Five Principles
**[~3 min]**

"So how do you walk forward with both hands full? Here are five principles.

**1. Use the tools while acknowledging the problems.** You can be critical AND engaged. Keep a kill switch. Human review is non-negotiable.

**2. Train AI on your own work.** You own the model. You own the output. The future is personalized, not generic slop.

**3. Become a selector.** DJs were originally called 'selectors.' Generation is cheap. Taste is not. Your taste is your moat.

**4. Write for the bot.** If your values aren't expressed in text, to AI they don't exist.

**5. Ship culture, not content.** Products commoditize. Culture compounds. Community outlasts product.

Let me go deeper on three of these."

## SLIDE 11b: Become a Selector — Remix Manifesto
**[~2-3 min]**

"20 years ago, one of my favorite films came out called RiP!: A Remix Manifesto. Has anyone here seen this movie by Brett Gaylor?

*[Wait for hands]*

It came out right at the beginning of the whole remix music, electronic dance music kind of revolution — samples, mashups, the copyright battles.

And it provided a lens for looking at copyright and changing tech as it related to music and art.

**The premise of the movie is this:**

*'Culture always builds on the past. The past always tries to control the future. Our future is becoming less free. To build free societies, we must limit the control of the past.'*

Sound familiar?

**Here we are again.** All our creative works have been slurped up into these AIs. Trained on the work of mankind, without consent, for profit.

But here's the thing — DJs navigated this exact tension. They were originally called 'selectors.' They didn't create from nothing — they curated, combined, remixed what already existed.

**That's our future role.** Generation is cheap. Taste is not. Your taste is your moat.

The people who thrived in remix culture weren't the ones who ignored it or the ones who fought it — they were the ones who learned to select."

## Q&A PREP — COMMON QUESTIONS

**"Is my degree worthless now?"**
No. Your training is the foundation. AI is the accelerator. But you need to augment it.

**"What about copyright?"**
Real problem. Real solutions emerging. Train on your own work. Philippe Pasquier's lab at SFU is doing this.

**"How do I actually start?"**
Pick one tool. One project. One week. Ship it ugly. Don't plan forever—build to learn.

**"Will clients devalue my work?"**
They care about quality and outcome, not whether you used AI to get there.

**THREE RULES FOR STARTING:**
1. **Map before you automate** — understand your workflow first. Don't automate chaos.
2. **10x or skip** — only adopt tools that are massively better, not incrementally better.
3. **You decide, AI assists** — you make the final call. Always.
