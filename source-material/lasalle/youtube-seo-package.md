## 📝 YouTube Description

"AI is trained on stolen work without consent. And I've never been more creative, productive, or powerful." Both things are true—here's how to navigate that contradiction.

🎯 **WHAT YOU'LL LEARN:**
- Why both the AI hype AND the AI doom takes are lazy
- The "both hands full" framework for creative professionals
- Real examples: designers compressing weeks into hours, VFX artists building new tools
- How to train AI on your own work (and why it matters)
- The difference between generation (cheap) and selection (valuable)
- Why self-knowledge is the prerequisite to AI mastery
- Practical homework: building your style guide, worldview doc, and creative DNA

🎤 **ABOUT THIS TALK:**
Kris Krüg delivers a keynote to ~100 LaSalle College Vancouver alumni (animation, game design, graphic design, fashion, interior design, recording arts). This isn't another "AI is amazing" or "AI will destroy us" talk—it's a practical framework for holding critique and curiosity simultaneously while building creative work that matters.

Kris shares his journey from finding 1,800 of his photographs in AI training datasets to winning an Ars Electronica honorable mention for research training models on his own portrait photography. Along the way: live audience Q&A on everything from classroom assessment to VFX pipeline overwhelm to water consumption concerns.
