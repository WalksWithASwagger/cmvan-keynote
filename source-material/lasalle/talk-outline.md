## Four Principles

1. **Use the tools while acknowledging the problems** — Critical AND engaged. Keep a kill switch.
2. **Train AI on your own work** — You own the model. You own the output.
3. **Become a selector** — Generation is cheap. Taste is not.
4. **Write for the bot** — If your values aren't in text, to AI they don't exist.

## Q&A Prep

**Common questions:**
- "Is my degree worthless?" — No, but augment it. Degree = taste, judgment, process. AI = execution.
- "What about copyright?" — Real problem. Train on your own work. Advocate for policy.
- "How do I start?" — Pick one tool, one project, one week. Ship it ugly.
- "Will clients devalue my work?" — They care about quality and timeline, not method.

**3 Rules for Starting:**
- Map before you automate
- 10x or skip
- You decide, AI assists
