## Opening: "What If I'm Not A Writer?" (300 words)

**Week 2, Coaching Session:**

Vicki (visual artist). Portfolio gap. Uncertain about career.

**Her Question:**

> "I'm a visual person, not a writer. I know we're supposed to document our thinking, but... what if I don't have words for what I do? What if my strength can't be explained?"

**The Fear Underneath:**

"If AI requires me to articulate my process, and my process is visual/intuitive, then I can't use AI effectively."

**My Response:**

> "Okay. Upload 20 of your favorite photos. Ask AI: 'What patterns do you see in my work? What choices am I making consistently?'"

**She did.**

**The AI's Analysis:**

> "Your photos seem impromptu but there's deep intentionality. The connection between photographer and subject reveals itself in the eyes—that's where meaning concentrates. Your color palette trends toward warm earth tones with singular cool accent. Your composition follows rule of thirds but breaks it for emotional emphasis..."

**Vicki's Reaction:**

Pause. Then:

> "It... it sees things I didn't know I was doing. I DO care about eyes. I DO use that color approach. I thought it was just intuition, but it's actually a pattern."

**The Revelation:**

She had taste. Deep, sophisticated taste.

She just hadn't ARTICULATED it before.

**AI became the mirror showing her own patterns.**

**The Insight:**

You don't need to "be a writer" to have a process worth documenting.

You just need to LET AI SHOW YOU the patterns you're already making.

**That's not replacing your expertise. That's revealing it to you.**

## Section 1: Generation Is Cheap, Selection Is Not (400 words)

**The Selector Framework (From RiP: A Remix Manifesto):**

Documentary about mashup culture, remix rights, creative theft.

**The Line That Stuck:**

> "Culture always builds on the past. The past tries to control the future."

DJs navigated this tension. They were called **selectors.**

Not creators. Selectors.

**Why That Word Matters:**

2005: Generation was hard, selection was easy
- Hard to make music → Easy to choose what to play

2025: Reversed
- Easy to generate anything → Hard to choose what's actually good

**The Shift:**

DJ was curator. Now everyone is.

**The New Scarcity:**

Not: "Can you generate?"
Everyone can. Midjourney, ChatGPT, Sora—commodity generation.

But: "Can you SELECT?"
Can you look at 10 AI outputs and explain why THIS one, not THAT one?

**Most people can't.**

They know what they like. They can't explain WHY.

**That's the problem.**

**The Taste Gap:**

Person A: "I like this design."
Person B: "Why?"
Person A: "I just... do. It feels right."

**That's intuition. Valuable. But not transferable.**

If you can't articulate why you chose it, you can't:
- Teach a team to make same choice
- Give AI guidance to replicate it
- Defend your decision to stakeholders
- Scale your judgment

**Intuition that stays internal = Bottleneck.**

**The Excavation:**

AI forces you to externalize your taste.

Not because AI needs it (though it does). Because SCALING needs it.

**Vicki's realization:**

"I thought my intuition was magic. Actually it's pattern recognition I've developed over 10 years. AI helped me SEE the patterns."

**Once you see them, you can teach them.**

**That's the moat.**

## Section 2: The Four Documents That Externalize Your Taste (550 words)

**The Creative DNA Excavation Process:**

### Document 1: Writing Style Guide

**The Exercise:**

Analyze 5-10 pieces you're proud of. Ask AI:

"What patterns do you see in how I write?"

**Example Output:**

> "You write in short sentences. Active voice dominant. Conversational tone but not sloppy. You use 'yo' and 'folks' frequently. You avoid corporate jargon, buzzwords, and emojis. You lead with story, end with takeaway. Average sentence: 12-15 words. Paragraph length: 2-4 sentences."

**The Realization:**

"Oh. THAT'S my voice. I didn't know I had such consistent patterns."

**Now you can teach AI (or humans) to match it.**

### Document 2: Worldview Document

**The Exercise:**

Answer these:
- What do I believe about my craft?
- What matters most to me in my work?
- What am I trying to achieve beyond deliverables?
- What do I refuse to compromise?

**Example (Designer):**

> "I believe design should be accessible, not just beautiful. Every choice should serve the user's goals, not my portfolio. I will not sacrifice usability for aesthetics. I care about people using the thing successfully more than winning awards."

**The Realization:**

Your values SHAPE your decisions. If AI doesn't know your values, it can't make decisions aligned with them.

### Document 3: Glossary + Anti-Glossary

**Glossary: Words you use constantly**

Example:
- "Folks" (not "guys" or "people")
- "Build" (not "develop" or "create")
- "Ship" (not "launch" or "release")
- "Systems" (not "hacks" or "tips")

**Anti-Glossary: Words you never use**

Example:
- Never: "Leverage" (as verb)
- Never: "Synergy"
- Never: "Circle back"
- Never: "Low-hanging fruit"

**The Effect:**

AI now knows your language identity.

Outputs sound like YOU, not corporate memo.

### Document 4: Decision Logs

**The Practice:**

When you make a choice (design A over B, word X over Y, approach 1 over 2):

Document WHY.

**Example:**

> "Chose serif font over sans-serif because this project is about tradition and trust. Sans-serif felt too modern/tech for the emotional tone we needed."

**After 10 decisions logged:**

You have a PATTERN of how you think.

**Feed that to AI. It learns your decision-making framework.**

**Vicki's Example:**

After documenting 15 photo selections:

> "I always choose the one where subject's eyes show vulnerability, even if composition is slightly worse. Eyes > perfection. That's my actual taste."

**Now AI can help her SELECT photos using that criterion.**

**The Moat:**

Anyone can generate 1,000 photos.

Vicki can SELECT the one that matters. And explain why.

**That's irreplaceable.**

## Section 3: Why Most Creatives Have Never Done This Work (350 words)

**The Success-Despite Pattern:**

Most creatives have been successful DESPITE never articulating their taste.

They just:
- "Know" what's good
- "Feel" what works
- "Recognize" quality instantly

**Intuition worked fine when:**
- You were the only one doing the work
- Clients trusted your judgment without explanation
- No one needed to replicate your decisions

**AI makes this impossible.**

**The Mirror Effect:**

Ask AI "write me something good" without context →  Corporate word salad

Why?

Because AI doesn't know what "good" means to YOU.

**Your options:**

**Option 1:** Blame AI. "It doesn't get my style."

**Option 2:** Realize you've never articulated your style clearly enough for ANYONE (human or AI) to replicate.

**Most people pick Option 1. Stay frustrated.**

**The students who transform pick Option 2:**

"Oh. I need to externalize what I know intuitively. Not for AI—for ME."

**Simon's Example (Sales Leader):**

15 years doing lead qualification. "I just know" which leads are good.

**Tried to document for AI:**

Realized he couldn't explain it.

**The Process:**

Weeks 3-5: Documents framework (12 criteria he was using unconsciously)

**Result:**

Now he can train junior reps in a week (vs. year-long apprenticeship before).

**AI didn't replace his expertise. It forced him to MAKE IT TRANSFERABLE.**

**The Meta-Skill:**

Externalizing taste isn't just for AI.

It's for:
- Training teams
- Scaling your judgment
- Becoming valuable at management level (not just execution)

**AI is the forcing function. But the benefit extends everywhere.**

## Section 4: The Taste Audit (How To Discover Your Own Patterns) (450 words)

**The Exercise I Give Students:**

### Step 1: Gather Your Best Work (10-20 examples)

What are you PROUD of?

Not: What performed well
But: What represents your taste at its best

### Step 2: Upload to AI

"Analyze these. What patterns do you see in my choices, style, approach?"

### Step 3: Read The Analysis

**Look for surprises.**

Things AI sees that you didn't know you were doing.

**Vicki:** "Connection in eyes"
**Designer student:** "You break symmetry intentionally for tension"
**Writer student:** "You end every piece with a question"

### Step 4: Validate or Refine

AI's analysis will be ~70% accurate.

**Your job:**
- What's true? (Keep that)
- What's wrong? (Correct it)
- What's missing? (Add it)

### Step 5: Create Your One-Page Taste Profile

**Format:**

"I care about [what matters most].
I prioritize [hierarchy of values].
I always [consistent patterns].
I never [anti-patterns].
When choosing between X and Y, I pick X when [criteria]."

**Example (Photographer):**

> "I care about human connection more than technical perfection. I prioritize emotional truth over aesthetic beauty. I always include eyes (even if it means worse composition). I never over-process skin tones. When choosing between sharp-but-cold and soft-but-warm, I pick warm when the subject is vulnerable, sharp when they're confident."

**Now AI knows your taste framework.**

### Step 6: Test It

Give AI a task using your taste profile.

See if output matches your judgment.

**Refine until it does.**

**The Result:**

You've externalized your taste.

Now it's:
- Teachable
- Scalable
- Valuable beyond your personal execution

**That's the moat.**

## Closing: If You Can't Articulate It, You Don't Own It (200 words)

**The Harsh Truth:**

"I just know what's good" isn't expertise. It's unexamined habit.

**Real expertise:**

"I know what's good AND I can explain why."

**The Difference:**

Unexamined: Can't teach it. Can't scale it. Can't defend it. Locked in your head.

Examined: Can teach teams. Can guide AI. Can explain to clients. Becomes organizational asset.

**AI forces the examination.**

Not to replace you. To REVEAL you to yourself.

**Vicki started thinking her intuition made her irreplaceable.**

**She was half right.**

Her intuition IS valuable. But **unexplained intuition** can't be leveraged.

**Articulated taste = Explained intuition = Scalable expertise.**

**Your Challenge:**

Do the taste audit this week.

Upload your 10 best pieces. Ask AI: "What patterns do you see?"

**I bet:**

You'll discover you have taste you didn't know you could explain.

**That's the moat.**

Generation is cheap. Taste is not.

**Make yours explicit.**
