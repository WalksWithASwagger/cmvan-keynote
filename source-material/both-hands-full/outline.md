# Both Hands Full: How To Hold Critique And Curiosity Simultaneously

**Word Target:** 2,000 words
**Opening Style:** The moment of non-consent (visceral, personal)
**Framework:** The binary trap → left hand (critique) → right hand (curiosity) → walking forward
**Evidence:** LaSalle College keynote content + student reactions + personal testimony
**Closing:** The choice (engage or sideline?)

---

## Opening: I Didn't Ask For This (300 words)

**The Line That Stops Rooms:**

> "I find my relationship with AI completely non-consensual."

LaSalle College, January 2026. 200 art and design students.

Silence.

> "It didn't ask me to steal my work, to take over this stuff, to change everything around me. All I'm trying to do is keep my head above water and reinvent myself."

**The Feeling:**

One day you're building a career through craft. Years of mastering your tools, developing your eye, earning your reputation.

Next day: Someone's selling your style for $20/month. Subscribe to "draw like you" as a service.

**You didn't choose this.**

Nobody asked: "Hey, can we train AI on all your work?"

Nobody said: "This will fundamentally alter your industry—cool with that?"

It just... happened.

**The Non-Consensual Moment:**

That's what this is. A moment that didn't ask permission.

And here's the thing: **You can be pissed about that AND still engage.**

Both are true.

**The Binary Trap:**

Most conversations about AI force you to pick:

**The Boosters:** "AI is amazing! Embrace it fully! Stop being a luddite!"

**The Doomers:** "AI is destroying everything! Resist! Don't be complicit!"

**Both camps say:** Pick a side.

**I'm saying:** **What if you don't?**

What if you hold the critique in your left hand—AI IS trained on stolen work, consent WAS violated, juniors pipelines ARE collapsing—

AND hold curiosity in your right hand—designers ARE compressing weeks into hours, new capabilities ARE unlocked, transformation IS happening—

**And keep walking forward with both hands full?**

This article is about that practice.

## Section 1: Left Hand - The Critique (The Five Fears) (500 words)

**What's Actually True:**

### Fear 1: Stolen Work Without Consent

Training data = Humanity's creative output scraped without permission.

Your photos. Your writing. Your code. Your art.

Used without asking. No compensation. No attribution.

**This is theft.** Call it what it is.

### Fear 2: Race To The Bottom

"Good enough" AI outputs devalue human craft.

Why pay $5,000 for a designer when Midjourney costs $20/month?

Why hire a writer when ChatGPT writes "adequate" copy?

**Market pressure toward commodification.**

### Fear 3: Junior Pipeline Collapse

Entry-level jobs vanishing. The roles that trained seniors are disappearing.

Peter's example: Ivy League grad can't find first job. Entry work done by AI now.

**How do people become seniors if they can't be juniors first?**

Nobody knows.

### Fear 4: Cognitive Dependency

If you outsource thinking, do you atrophy?

Use calculator daily → Mental math degrades
Use GPS constantly → Spatial memory weakens
Use AI for everything → Does critical thinking diminish?

**The muscle atrophy problem.** Real concern.

### Fear 5: Commodification

If anyone can generate anything, why do they need YOU?

Expertise devalued. Craft dismissed. "We'll just use AI" becomes the answer.

**This is the fear underneath all others:** Becoming obsolete.

**What I Tell Students:**

All five fears are LEGITIMATE.

I'm not dismissing them. I'm not saying "it'll be fine."

**I'm saying: These risks are real. AND you still have agency.**

**The Binary Trap:**

If you acknowledge these fears, you're expected to RESIST AI completely.

That's the trap.

**You can see the problems clearly AND engage anyway.**

Critique doesn't require withdrawal.

**The Left Hand:** Hold these fears. Don't drop them. Stay critical.

They keep you honest.

**Evidence to Pull:**
- Five fears framework from LaSalle keynote
- Student additions (environmental impact, emotional content, etc.)
- Junior pipeline data (Ivy League example)
- Each fear grounded in real evidence
- My validation: "These are legitimate"

## Section 3: Walking Forward (Both Hands Full) (400 words)

**The Practice:**

Not: Pick a side and commit
But: Hold both sides and keep moving

**What This Looks Like Day-to-Day:**

**Morning:** Build using AI. Compress 4 hours into 30 minutes.

**Afternoon:** Teach about consent violations and ethical concerns.

**Evening:** Advocate for alternative models (Metacreation Lab, local training, artist-owned data).

**All the same day. Not conflicting. All necessary.**

**The Student Who Got This:**

Emily (educator).

**Her practice:**
- Uses AI to build Bee School (right hand - engaging capability)
- Teaches kids about consent and data sovereignty (left hand - maintaining critique)
- Both in same curriculum

**Result:** Kids learn AI literacy AND ethical thinking. Not one or the other.

**The Community Who Got This:**

BC + AI structure:
- We USE AI (right hand - practical application)
- We CRITIQUE AI (left hand - Indigenous data sovereignty work, ethics subgroup)

**Both happening simultaneously.**

**The Alternative (What I Reject):**

**Pure Boosters:** "AI is all good! Adopt uncritically!"

- Becomes complicit in extraction
- No ethical grounding

**Pure Doomers:** "AI is all bad! Resist completely!"
- Loses agency
- Cedes governance to true believers
- No ability to shape outcomes

**Both feel clean. Both are incomplete.**

**The Messy Middle (Both Hands Full):**

"This is problematic AND I'm engaging anyway because opting out is worse."

**Uncomfortable. True.**

**The Moment Didn't Ask Permission:**

AI is here. Junior pipelines are collapsing. Your work is in training data.

You can:

**Option 1:** Embrace uncritically (become a booster)

**Option 2:** Resist completely (become a doomer)

**Option 3:** Walk forward with both hands full

**What Both Hands Full Means:**

Left hand (critique):
- This is non-consensual
- This is unjust
- This is extractive
- I'm not okay with it

Right hand (curiosity):
- This is transformative
- This creates capability
- This is happening
- I want agency in shaping it

**Both. Simultaneously. While moving forward.**

**The Test:**

Can you hold complexity like a grown-up?

Or do you need clean narratives?

**I'm choosing complexity.**

Because if all the ethical, critical, thoughtful people opt out—governance gets made by opportunists and true believers.

**That's worse.**

**So I'm here. Both hands full. Walking forward.**

**You coming?**
