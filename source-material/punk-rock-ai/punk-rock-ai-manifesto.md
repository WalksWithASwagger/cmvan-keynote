# The Punk Rock AI Manifesto

*Our philosophy for AI filmmaking at the AI Film Club*

---

## The Core Idea

There's something very punk rock about taking a tool that's staring you in the face and using it to tell the stories that matter.

Like punk rock emerging from Xerox copying machines, we're using the master's tools to tell stories of resistance, creativity, and human connection. We're not waiting for Hollywood's permission. We're not asking gatekeepers for access. We're building our own pipelines and telling our own stories.

That's Punk Rock AI.

---

## What This Means in Practice

### 1. The Technology Doesn't Care Who Uses It

AI democratizes the tools. That's the opportunity. What used to require $200,000 budgets and Hollywood connections now runs on a laptop and costs $100. The barrier isn't money anymore—it's whether you know how to use the tools and whether you have something to say.

Kevin Friel spent 25 years in Hollywood VFX. Now he teaches 1,500+ students to skip the gatekeepers entirely. That's punk rock. Access for everyone who wants to learn.

### 2. The Stories Still Have to Matter

AI doesn't give you ideas. It doesn't give you vision. It doesn't tell you what matters.

If you don't have something to say, AI just helps you say nothing faster.

The punk rock ethos isn't about the tools—it's about using whatever's available to amplify voices that wouldn't otherwise be heard. The technology is the means. The story is the point.

### 3. DIY or Die

Build your own workflow. Don't wait for someone to sell you theirs.

ComfyUI isn't just a tool—it's a philosophy. Node-based, customizable, shareable. When someone in the Film Club figures out a workflow on Tuesday, three people have improved it by Thursday. That's peer learning. That's DIY culture. That's punk rock.

We troubleshoot each other's setups at midnight. We share what works. We document what doesn't. No NDAs. No gatekeeping. Just filmmakers helping filmmakers.

### 4. Reject the Hype, Keep the Tools

The AI industry runs on hype. New tools every week. Revolutionary promises. Paradigm shifts. Most of it is noise.

Punk Rock AI means testing everything and keeping what works. We're not cheerleaders for any platform. We're filmmakers evaluating tools for real production. Client work. Festival submissions. Actual pipelines.

What works gets shared. What doesn't gets documented. No vendor allegiance. Just what serves the work.

### 5. Community Over Competition

The old model: protect your secrets, gatekeep your knowledge, compete for scarce opportunities.

The punk rock model: share everything, lift each other up, create abundance.

When Luke shows a $100 commercial that would've cost $200K traditionally, he's not hoarding that knowledge. He's showing everyone how he did it. When Kevin shares 25 years of VFX wisdom at monthly screenings, he's not charging consulting fees. He's building a community where everyone levels up.

That's how we win—together.

## The Manifesto

**We believe:**

- AI is a tool, not a replacement for human creativity
- Access to filmmaking tools should not require Hollywood connections
- Knowledge shared freely compounds capability for everyone
- Stories matter more than technology—always
- Building your own workflow beats buying someone else's
- Community beats competition
- Transparency about AI use builds trust
- The future of film is being written by whoever shows up to write it

**We reject:**

- Gatekeeping and artificial scarcity
- Hype over substance
- Technology worship
- Vendor allegiance over filmmaker needs
- Fear-based narratives about AI
- The idea that only Hollywood can make Hollywood-quality work

**We practice:**

- Monthly screenings with honest discussions
- Peer learning and workflow sharing
- Real production testing, not just demos
- Ethical filmmaking with disclosure when it matters
- Community building over personal brand building
- Showing up for each other
