# Vancouver AI Meetup #23 - Key Quotes Collection

**Event Date:** November 26, 2025
**Location:** HR MacMillan Space Centre, Vancouver, BC
**Compiled:** November 29, 2025

---

## Purpose

This document collects the most impactful, shareable, and significant quotes from Meetup #23. Organized by speaker and theme for easy reference in blog posts, social media, presentations, and community materials.

## Anthony Joseph (Isiléyu) - Squamish Nation Counselor

### On Technology Adoption
> "Ancestors didn't have chainsaws. But they would've used it if it was there."

### On Ancestral Naming
> "To get an ancestral name, your elders would watch you and see how you carry yourself."

### On Resilience
> "Humor is what got us through the darkest of times and humor is what will continue to catch us."

---

## Lionel Emba - WhatsApp AI / Energy Monitoring

### On Token Consumption Awards
> "What if we turn this onto its head and actually created an award for saving tokens?"

### On Impact Scale
> "1 trillion tokens equals the energy use of 52,000 households daily energy use. That's 1 trillion tokens or 3.5 million kilometers driven in a car."

### On Open Source Importance
> "Open source is really the reason why we know all this. There would be no data on this matter and Google probably wouldn't step forward to do this otherwise."

### On AI-Generated Vulnerability Reports
> "Only 5% of those reports are legitimate. So they're basically spending time on reports that are absolutely not reproducible. They're completely hallucinated."

---

## Amanda Silvera - Voice Actor / SOBIR Founder

### On Voice Theft Discovery
> "It was really crazy because it was so good that I'm sitting there and I'm like, did I say that? I absolutely did not."

### On Rights and Identity
> "I was hitting a wall when it came to my rights. I was hitting a wall when it came to how much of my voice is even mine."

### On Human Responsibility
> "It's not AI that's doing this. It's people. It's people abusing something."

### On Balance
> "AI can do amazing things. It's a wonderful tool and there are so many ways that we can use it. There has to be balance."

### On Future Protection
> "We all want this protection, man, woman, and child. It may be rare right now, but it won't be rare for long."

---

## Kay Tigo - Montreal AI Ethics Institute

### On Humanity's Position
> "The problem with humanity is that we have paleolithic emotions with medieval institutions and godlike technology."
> *(Quoting biologist Edward Wilson)*

### On Policy Failure
> "Policy fails when people who are most affected, who have to live with the consequences, are not included in conversations that will affect their lives."

### On Rushing Technology
> "We failed to implement thoughtfully if we rush to deploy new technologies."

### On Human Development
> "Go to therapy, deal with your trauma. Can we get real about that? Like for real? If you don't have enough empathy, then what's wrong with you?"

### On AI and Theft
> "The entire thing is built on theft."

### On Crossroads
> "Humanity is at a crossroads and how we proceed will determine whether we're doomed or redeemed."

### On Self-Replacement
> "I just don't quite understand the reason why we're so obsessed with replacing ourselves."

### On Accountability
> "It's about transparency, it's about accountability, and it boils down to consent. It boils down to control and compensation."

## [[Kevin Friel]] - AI Filmmaker

### On Nvidia Monopoly
> "We have one major player who is providing compute on the GPU scale to everyone - the $5 trillion elephant in the room, Nvidia."

### On Canadian Opportunity
> "We really have an amazing opportunity as Canadians to actually lead that. We have so much history in companies like Nortel and TeleSat and Blackberry."

### On Compute Carbon Offset
> "It's almost like a carbon offset situation. It's a compute carbon offset."

### On Punk Rock AI
> "There's something very punk rock about having something that's staring you in the face and using that tool in and of itself to tell the stories, to create the change."

### On Xerox and Punk
> "Punk rock. The aesthetic came to life because of the Xerox copying machine."

### On Using the Tools
> "Let's use the tools that are here to tell stories that remind us exactly what we're here for, what we're fighting for - our families, our communities, our global village."

## Liz Marshall - Documentary Filmmaker

### On AI and Water
> "Is AI draining this world for another?"

### On Data Center Water Use
> "By 2027 data centers will guzzle up 1.7 trillion gallons of water per year."

### On Canadian Water
> "We have 20% of the world's fresh water, yet only 7% of that is renewable."

### On Indigenous Water Rights
> "There are 36 indigenous nations that are still living without drinkable, potable clean water."

### On Water Justice
> "Water belongs to the public commons. It belongs to the earth, it belongs to future generations. It belongs to all species, it belongs to nature, and it belongs exactly where nature put the water."

### On CASK Framework
> "Curiosity, Awareness, Skepticism and Caution. That's specifically about AI."

### On Resistance
> "The resistance has arrived."

### On Protecting Earth
> "Let's reinvigorate and not forget the movement, the human based movement to protect this place."

## Catherine Warren - FanTrust / Panel Moderator

### On Vancouver's Position
> "Vancouver is a global powerhouse when it comes to media and entertainment. We're also the home of Adbusters, Greenpeace, and many other action oriented media and activist organizations."

### On Intelligence vs. Will
> "We don't need intelligence, human or artificial to address the climate emergency. It's a problem of political will."

### On Netflix Carbon Footprint
> "Netflix, just one streaming company, its annual carbon footprint is about 1.6 million tons of CO2, which is roughly equivalent to all the yearly emissions of the city of Vancouver."

### On Vancouver's Advantage
> "Vancouver is a global center for circularity and for the B Corp movement. So we already have these models that are capitalist busting right here at our doorstep."

---

## Topo - UBC Computer Scientist

### On Economic Systems
> "When my students ask me, 'Hey Topo, what do we have to design? What's the best thing we can design?' I always tell them, it's not an app, it's not a website, it's none of that. Design a new economic system."

### On Root Causes
> "This is not a technology problem. It's not even a political problem, it's an economic problem."

### On Being Bold
> "I am a Luddite, I am a socialist. And I think that we need to be able to say these things without fear."

### On Democratization
> "Democratization is about participation. And if that's the case, then the Egyptian Pharaoh democratized pyramid building."

### On Developer Laziness
> "We got lazy as developers because back in the nineties, optimization was the key."
