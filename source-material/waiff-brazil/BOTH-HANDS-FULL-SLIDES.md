# Both Hands Full -- Slide Deck Outline

**Total Slides:** 25
**Design System:** DIY punk zine. Collage. Handmade. Hardcore. Photocopied aesthetic. NOT corporate. NOT polished. Looks like it was assembled at 3am with scissors, glue, a Xerox machine, and something to say.
**Aspect Ratio:** 16:9
**Palette:** BLACK, WHITE, RED. Nothing else. Ever.

---

## DESIGN BIBLE

### The Aesthetic: Punk Zine Collage

Think Crass Records sleeve art. Dischord Records insert. Riot Grrrl zine taped to a bathroom wall. Brazilian Tropicalia album art meets Sao Paulo street paste-ups. The slides should look like they were made by hand, photocopied badly, and stapled together with conviction.

This is not a TED deck. This is not a startup pitch. This is a zine projected at 30 feet wide for 600 filmmakers in Sao Paulo -- on the road to Cannes. It should feel dangerous, honest, and handmade.

### Colors -- THREE ONLY

| Color | Use | Feeling |
|-------|-----|---------|
| **BLACK** | Backgrounds, heavy type, borders, ink splatter | Weight. Authority. The dark room before the projector hits. |
| **WHITE** | Text, cut-out shapes, negative space, Xerox grain | Raw truth. The page. The light. |
| **RED** | Accents, strikes, underlines, blood, urgency, the hand | Anger. Love. Aliveness. The only color that matters. |

No gradients. No transparency. No pastels. If it can't be screenprinted in a garage, it doesn't belong here.

### Typography

- **Headlines:** Heavy bold sans-serif OR hand-drawn blocky caps. Imperfect. Slightly crooked. Like rubber-stamped or stenciled. Think Black Flag logo energy.
- **Quotes:** Typewriter font. Courier. Monospaced. Like it was hammered out on an actual machine. Or handwritten in thick marker.
- **Data/Stats:** Stencil font. Military surplus meets protest sign. The numbers should feel urgent, not corporate.
- **Body text (where needed):** Cut-and-paste ransom note style OR clean typewriter. Never both on the same slide.
- **Portuguese subtitles:** Handwritten below English text. Different hand. Like a friend translated it in the margin.

### Textures & Techniques

- **Xerox grain:** Every slide should have some photocopy noise. Not clean. Degraded. Like it's been copied 10 times.
- **Cut-and-paste collage:** Text and images should look physically cut out and glued onto the background. Visible edges. Tape marks. Glue residue.
- **Hand marks:** Crossed-out text done BY HAND with thick marker, not a clean strikethrough font. Underlines are jagged. Circles are imperfect.
- **Ink splatter / spray paint:** Occasional. Not on every slide. When something needs to feel raw.
- **High contrast photos:** If photos are used, they're black and white, high contrast, halftone dot pattern. Xeroxed to hell. Grainy. Like a flyer for a basement show.
- **Red as punctuation:** Red appears sparingly. A single red word. A red X. A red underline. A red hand print. When red shows up, it MEANS something.

### What This Is NOT

- Not minimalist tech aesthetic
- Not startup pitch deck
- Not TED talk clean
- Not infographic
- Not corporate anything
- Not "design-forward" -- it's "message-forward"
- Not trying to be pretty. Trying to be TRUE.

### DIY PRODUCTION METHOD

Ideally, build several slides as PHYSICAL COLLAGES:
1. Cut and paste text and images on actual paper
2. Photocopy them (badly, on purpose)
3. Scan or photograph the photocopies
4. Place into presentation software as full-bleed images

This gives authentic texture no Photoshop filter can replicate. The grain is real because the process is real.

For slides that need to be digital (stats, text-heavy): use the typewriter/stencil fonts but add Xerox noise overlay in post. Scan a blank photocopy and use it as a texture layer on every slide.

## PRODUCTION NOTES

- **Transitions:** HARD CUT. No dissolves. No fades. Punk doesn't fade. Exception: Slide 23 (invocation) can fade to black slowly.
- **Font size:** HUGE. 600 people in the back row. If in doubt, make it bigger. Punk flyers are readable from across the street.
- **Text per slide:** Maximum 3 lines. Ideally 1. The fewer words, the harder they hit.
- **Backgrounds:** Mostly BLACK. White for Act II energy shift. Black returns for soul dimension and closing.
- **No animations:** Nothing moves. Nothing spins. Nothing slides in. The image appears. It hits. Next.
- **Portuguese:** Handwritten Portuguese translations below English on key slides (Both/And statement, Belgium quote, Irreducible List, "Your perspective" line). Different handwriting. Like a friend grabbed the pen and added it.
- **Aspect ratio consideration:** Some slides may work better as full-bleed scanned collages that break the 16:9 grid slightly -- torn edges bleeding off frame.

## THE PHILOSOPHY

This deck should look like it was made by someone who has something urgent to say and didn't have time to make it pretty. Because that's the truth. The content is too important for polish. The message is too raw for gradients.

Every filmmaker in that room has made a zine, or wanted to. Every one of them knows the difference between a corporate deck and something someone made because they couldn't NOT make it.

This deck says: I made this with my hands. I have something to tell you. It's not clean because the truth isn't clean.

Black. White. Red. Scissors. Glue. Xerox machine. Go.
