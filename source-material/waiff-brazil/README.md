## Overview

This directory contains materials for Kris Krug's keynote at the World AI Film Festival (WAIFF) Sao Paulo edition. The talk evolved from two sources: "Creative Rebellion in an Age of Synthetic Everything" (originally developed for EA executives, Dec 2025) and "Both Hands Full" (the signature keynote on holding critique and curiosity simultaneously). The synthesis leads with vulnerability and story over frameworks, includes character-driven transformation arcs, integrates the soul dimension from Bass Coast, and ends with a performed invocation rather than rhetorical questions.

## Core Philosophy (Unchanged from EA Talk)

### The Big Question
How do you stay creative and bring humanity to your work when AI can generate anything?

### The Answer
You don't compete with machines. You build ecosystems of interconnected creative entities that require care, attention, and human vision over time. You rebel creatively - not against technology, but against the soulless application of it.

### Key Concepts

**Ecosystem Thinking vs. Product Thinking:**
- Not one perfect film - many interconnected projects
- Not individual achievement - community building
- Not extracting value - creating culture
- Not optimizing for metrics - optimizing for meaning
- Not sprint mentality - marathon mentality

**The Mycelial Network:**
Like a forest, your visible work (films) is supported by underground networks (relationships, community, ongoing learning, collaborations). Tend to the whole ecosystem, not just the visible product.

**Human Elements AI Can't Replace:**
1. Cultural context and lived experience
2. Curation and taste
3. Intention and meaning
4. Relationships and collaboration
5. Values and vision

## Cultural Considerations for Brazil

### Brazilian Film Context

**Filmmakers to Reference:**
- **Cinema Novo Movement:** Glauber Rocha, Nelson Pereira dos Santos (ecosystem builders)
- **Contemporary Auteurs:** Kleber Mendonça Filho, Anna Muylaert, Karim Aïnouz
- **Industry:** Rede Globo (infrastructure), independent film tradition

**Why This Matters:**
- Shows respect for Brazilian cinema tradition
- Demonstrates that ecosystem thinking has Brazilian precedent
- Makes talk locally relevant, not just North American import

### Values Alignment

**Brazilian Cultural Values:**
- Community and family (ecosystem thinking resonates)
- Joy and celebration (not all serious)
- Resistance and creativity (Cinema Novo tradition)
- Warmth and connection (relational, not transactional)

**How Talk Reflects These:**
- Emphasizes community over individual achievement
- Includes moments of humor and celebration
- Connects to Brazilian cinema's political tradition
- Warm, personal tone, not just professional

### What to Avoid

**Don't:**
- Position as "North American teaching Brazil"
- Assume Hollywood is the model to follow
- Be patronizing about AI adoption in Brazil
- Ignore Brazil's significant film industry

**Instead:**
- "Sharing what we learned in Vancouver, eager to learn from you"
- Recognize Brazil's unique position: major industry + independent spirit
- Acknowledge Brazilian filmmakers already using AI tools
- Celebrate Brazilian cinema tradition and innovation
