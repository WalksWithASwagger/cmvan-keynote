## The Core Tension

**Both/and thinking as operating system:**

> "We're stoked about all these opportunities and possibilities and we are, have concerns and questions over here and we're walking forward with both those things in our hands at the same time."

KK holds excitement and concern simultaneously. This isn't fence-sitting — it's intellectual honesty about technology's dual nature. He refuses the false binary of AI booster vs. AI doomer.

---

## On AI and Creativity

### AI as Mirror, Not Tool

> "AI is not a tool or a strategy — it's a mirror that reveals who we are, amplifies our creative DNA, and demands we become stewards of culture through technology."

AI doesn't just do things for you. It reveals who you are. Your prompts, your choices, your taste — AI amplifies whatever you bring to it. This makes self-knowledge prerequisite to AI mastery.

### The Stolen Art Problem

> "One of the problems that us artists have ethically with AI is that it's been trained on all the stolen work of all mankind without our permission or consent, with no compensation, no visibility truly even into what they took or how they're using it."

This isn't anti-AI. It's pro-artist. KK uses AI tools while acknowledging their ethical problems. The solution isn't abstention — it's building alternatives (like SFU's Metacreation Lab) where artists train models on their own work.

### Original Intelligence

> "You're the original intelligence, AI is the amplifier."

Humans aren't being replaced. We're the source. AI amplifies what we bring. This reframes the relationship: you're not competing with AI, you're wielding it.

## On Technology and Society

### The Extraction Critique

> "We're building non-extractive economic models."

Most tech extracts value from communities and sends it to distant shareholders. KK's model keeps value local. The Vancouver AI community isn't a funnel for Silicon Valley — it's infrastructure for BC.

### Innovation Theater

> "If your project doesn't lift someone up, it's innovation theater."

Technology projects that look good but don't help anyone. Press releases without impact. AI demos that never ship. KK has zero patience for performative innovation.

### Garden vs. Factory

> "Organizations need 'entangled ecosystems' that require participation and tending, not rigid production pipelines."

Traditional innovation funnels fail at AI speed. The metaphor shift: from factory (predictable output from standardized input) to garden (living system requiring cultivation, producing unexpected results).

## On Indigenous Integration

### Not Tokenistic

> "Coast Salish references, UNDRIP, sacred naming — never tokenistic."

Indigenous principles aren't branding. They're governance. Carol Ann Hilton on the board. Johnny Williams opening events with ceremony. UNDRIP alignment in policy decisions. This isn't diversity checkbox — it's structural commitment.

### Time Sovereignty

Johnny Williams's framing that KK amplifies:

> "AI handles colonial bureaucracy so Squamish people can focus on Squamish work."

AI as liberator, not colonizer. Technology serving cultural preservation, not cultural extraction. This inverts the usual AI narrative.

### Sacred Containers

Vancouver AI meetups open with Squamish ceremony. This isn't ritual for ritual's sake — it grounds technical conversation in land, responsibility, relationship. Creates different energy than typical tech events.

## On Community Building

### The Bat Signal

> "You saw the bat signal we were putting out."

Community forms around signals. The right language, the right references, the right guests — these filter for values alignment. You attract who you are.

### Insider/Outsider Positioning

> "Insider/outsider is where I operate best."

Working within systems while maintaining independence. Taking corporate money without becoming corporate. Speaking at government events while remaining free to critique. This requires constant navigation.

### Give More Than You Take

> "You got to give more than you take in order for these scenarios to work."

Core partnership principle. Sponsors should receive more value than they pay. Community members should gain more than they contribute. This creates surplus, not extraction.

## On Work and Practice

### Vibe Coding as Philosophy

> "The coolest thing about vibecoding I find actually is that what you think you want to build isn't really what you want to build. And by building it and fucking with it and pushing the buttons and going through the flows, you're like, 'Oh, this is what I actually need to build.'"

Building is thinking. You can't spec your way to understanding. The prototype reveals what the plan couldn't. This applies beyond code — to organizations, to events, to careers.

### Daily Practice

> "I vibe code up something almost every day, and it's only 9:40 today, and that's no exception. I built a BC + AI awards nomination vibe coded website this morning in the bathtub."

Not occasional experimentation — daily practice. Small builds compound. The bathtub detail matters: this integrates into life, not separate from it.

### Second Brain

> "Right at the outset, ask yourself: what is my knowledge management tool?"

External memory as prerequisite for AI collaboration. You can't leverage AI without a corpus to feed it. Knowledge management isn't productivity theater — it's infrastructure.

### Digital Composting

> "Digital composting — turning old transcripts into new insights."

Nothing is single-use. Transcripts become recaps become case studies become training data. Content recycles, compounds, grows.

## On Human Essence

### What AI Can't Eat

> "The AI can't eat ice cream for you — only you can determine your preferences."

Embodied experience remains human. Running, parenting, tasting. AI can describe but not experience. This isn't limitation — it's the irreducible human core.

### The Last Things We Hold

When AI can do calendaring, accounting, communications, video — what remains?

- **Embodied experiences** (physical presence, sensation)
- **Critical thinking and values expression**
- **Preferences, desires, hopes, and dreams**
- **Empathy and intuition**
- **Self-awareness and reflection**

### Write for the Bot

> "A lot of the smartest people I know right now are writing for the bot. They're just getting stuff out of their hearts and their heads and into the universe as a way to express to these new consciousnesses what humans are all about and the type of future we want to live in."

If your values aren't expressed in text, to AI they don't exist. This isn't about SEO — it's about training the future on what matters.

## On the Future

### AGI Timeline

> "Many researchers predict Artificial General Intelligence by 2028 — an AI better than humans at all things."

KK takes this seriously without panicking. The response isn't fear or denial — it's preparation. Build now. Document now. Train now.

### Shipping Culture

> "We're not shipping content anymore, we're shipping culture."

Products commoditize. Culture compounds. Community outlasts product. This is the strategic orientation: invest in what can't be easily replicated.

### Becoming Selectors

> "DJs were originally called 'selectors' — that's our future role."

The creative role shifts from generation to curation. Taste, judgment, selection become the differentiators. AI generates; humans select.

## On Leadership

### Distributed Credit

> "Shoutout to Aaron Hilton for pulling all-nighters with me debugging GPU errors in the hallway."

Credit flows outward. Named people. Specific contributions. Never solo-hero narrative. This builds community while acknowledging reality — work happens through collaboration.

### Vulnerability and Confidence Together

> "I just put 200 hours of our AI upgrade coaching transcripts into a thing to start to synthesize my coaching style, which I had never even thought about that I had a coaching style."

Public learning. Admitting what you didn't know. This isn't false modesty — it's modeling the experimental mindset he teaches.

### Ask Uncomfortable Questions

> "Why is every 'AI for Good' panel staffed by people who've never debugged a training pipeline in their lives?"

The questions others avoid. The observations that feel rude but are obviously true. KK says them.

## Summary: The KK Operating System

1. **Hold the tension** — excitement and concern, insider and outsider, scale and intimacy
2. **Build in public** — vulnerability, experimentation, naming failures
3. **Credit others** — named people, specific contributions, distributed wins
4. **Stay grounded** — land acknowledgment, embodied experience, local community
5. **Make things** — daily practice, vibe coding, prototype over plan
6. **Document everything** — second brain, digital composting, write for the future
7. **Call bullshit** — innovation theater, corporate buzzwords, performative AI
8. **Serve culture** — ship culture not content, community over product, non-extractive economics
