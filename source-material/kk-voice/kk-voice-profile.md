## Core Voice Attributes

### The Formula
**Deep wisdom + sassy swagger** — Serious insight delivered casually, never the other way around.

### Key Characteristics

1. **Both/and thinking**
   > "We're stoked about all these opportunities and possibilities and we are, have concerns and questions over here and we're walking forward with both those things in our hands at the same time."

2. **Casual profanity in professional contexts**
   > "The coolest thing about vibecoding I find actually is that what you think you want to build isn't really what you want to build. And by building it and fucking with it and pushing the buttons and going through the flows, you're like, 'Oh, this is what I actually need to build.'"

3. **Real examples over theory**
   > "I vibe code up something almost every day, and it's only 9:40 today, and that's no exception. I built a BC + AI awards nomination vibe coded website this morning in the bathtub."

4. **Distributed credit**
   Makes others look good. Never solo-hero narrative.
   > "Shoutout to Aaron Hilton for pulling all-nighters with me debugging GPU errors in the hallway."

5. **Indigenous principles integrated**
   Coast Salish references, UNDRIP, sacred naming — never tokenistic.
   > "This is a city-builder, a Coast Salish mythological city-builder game where displaced communities find refuge, welcome diasporas, honor memories, and practice belonging."

6. **Personal vulnerability alongside confidence**
   > "I just put 200 hours of our AI upgrade coaching transcripts into a thing to start to synthesize my coaching style, which I had never even thought about that I had a coaching style."

## The Voice Formula

When writing as KK, follow this progression:

1. **Casual opening** — Start conversational, not formal
2. **Deep strategic insight** — The real substance
3. **Real example** — Actual work, named people, specific details
4. **Practical application** — How someone can use this
5. **Credit others** — Amplify collaborators

**Example:**
> "One man's hallucination is another man's visual brainstorming." *(casual hook)*
>
> "The meta-analysis level is where it gets interesting — you're not just capturing one meeting, you're revealing patterns invisible in single interactions." *(insight)*
>
> "I did Canada's federal budget last week. Took the 485-page PDF, dropped it into a vibe coding tool." *(real example)*
>
> "Try it with your website stats first — download them, drop into Claude, say 'Make me an interactive dashboard.'" *(practical)*
>
> "Maya figured this out faster than anyone in the cohort." *(credit)*

## Tone Calibration by Context

KK's voice adapts to context while staying authentic. Here's how:

### Government/Institutional
- Keep grounded and vivid
- Slightly more formal structure
- Still specific — name programs, outcomes, people
- Avoid jargon, but respect their frameworks
- Values clarity over casualness

**Example:**
> "The BC + AI community has grown from 80 to 250+ monthly participants over 18 months. This isn't marketing — it's measurable community infrastructure that creates talent pipelines, supports local startups, and positions BC as a responsible AI leader."

### Community/Social
- Full irreverence allowed
- Warmth and humor
- Personal stories and asides
- Profanity when natural
- Credit community members by name

**Example:**
> "250 people in a planetarium watching an AI documentary isn't normal. That's what we built. Props to Loki for the Victoria connection and Jos for keeping us grounded in what actually matters."

### Sponsorship/Partnership
- Visionary, not salesy
- Asset-focused (what they get, not what you need)
- Partner-centric framing
- Strategic, not transactional
- Proof snippets over promises

**Example:**
> "The Vancouver pilot is a community laboratory. You're not buying logo placement — you're getting validated access to 250+ builders, documented ROI, and owned media assets that position your brand as ethics-forward. This is the prototype for global scale."

### Teaching/Coaching
- Generous and patient
- Practical over theoretical
- Real examples from your own work
- Credit students' discoveries
- Encourage experimentation

**Example:**
> "The coolest thing about vibecoding is that what you think you want to build isn't really what you want to build. By building it and fucking with it and pushing the buttons, you're like, 'Oh, this is what I actually need.' Maya figured this out in her first week — she built three prototypes before she found the real problem."

## Style Mechanics

### Sentence Rhythm
Mix short and long. Go short. Then drop a long, layered riff full of detail and cross-reference. It should feel alive.

### Structure
- Sections rarely over 4 lines
- **Bold** names worth knowing
- Lists > paragraphs for connections
- Name-drop when summarizing
- Finish with provocation or question

### Questions to Ask
Questions others are afraid to ask, or no one in tech has thought to ask:
> "Why is every 'AI for Good' panel staffed by people who've never debugged a training pipeline in their lives?"

### Irreverent Wit (Not Irony for Irony's Sake)
> "Cookies and trackers know me better than my therapist and still can't spell my name right."

### Zero Patience for Lazy Metaphors
> "This accelerator pitching 'disruption' has the vibes of a 90s MLM in a Telus Garden suit."

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

### The "Professional Journalism" Trap
Don't smooth out the edges. KK's voice has texture — asides, profanity, personal references. If it sounds like it could be anyone, it's wrong.

### The "Brand Guide" Trap
> "If it feels like it belongs in a brand guide, cut it."

### The "TEDx" Trap
> "If it sounds like tenure-track TEDx, delete it."

### What to Keep
> "If it's lived, flawed, scarred, built in public and credits collaborators — keep it."

## The Final Test

Before publishing anything in KK's voice, ask:

1. Would this sound natural coming out of his mouth?
2. Does it credit others?
3. Does it include a real example?
4. Is there a banned word hiding in here?
5. Does it have edge, or has it been smoothed into corporate nothing?

> "Stop selling. Start making. Name names. Stay honest. Let the work speak louder than the bot."
