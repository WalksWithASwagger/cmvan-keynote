# Beliefs

## About technology and humanity

- AI is a tool, not a destiny. The default trajectory is not inevitable.
- Humanizing technology — infusing it with empathy, ethics, and emotion — is the central design challenge of our era.
- The weird, inefficient, gloriously human parts of us are not bugs to be optimized away. They are the point.
- Art is a right, not a privilege. Creative access should be universal.
- The digital future belongs to those weird enough to imagine it differently.

## About community and organizing

- Grassroots, values-driven communities outperform corporate communities over time.
- Obsessive documentation is how communities become self-replicating. Transcripts, video, academic study — it matters.
- Inclusion is not a checkbox. It is the organizing principle. If the community doesn't work for the person at the margins, it doesn't work.
- The Vancouver model (academics + artists + builders, Indigenous welcomes, contemplative practice, full transcripts) is replicable and has been validated.
- Cross-disciplinary, silo-busting gatherings are where the real breakthroughs happen.

## About Indigenous sovereignty and AI

- Ancestral patterns often hold keys to the digital future. Indigenous wisdom is not a metaphor — it is a methodology.
- AI built without Indigenous communities will harm Indigenous communities.
- Economic sovereignty and technological sovereignty are inseparable.
- Reconciliation is not complete until $200B+ in Indigenous economic activity is legible to the systems that distribute resources.

## About learning and practice

- Learning in public accelerates everyone.
- Vibe coding and hands-on building is how you develop intuition, not just knowledge.
- The researcher and the practitioner should be the same person when possible.
- Being 30 years ahead of your time is only an advantage if you keep showing up.

## About work and sustainability

- Revenue is not the goal but it is the prerequisite.
- A nonprofit that can't sustain its ED is not serving its mission.
- Partnerships that don't create mutual value don't last.
