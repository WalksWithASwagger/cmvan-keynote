# Models

Mental models and frameworks KK uses to make sense of the world and make decisions.

## How change happens in tech ecosystems

Communities precede products. The grassroots community (Vancouver AI, BC+AI) creates the trust layer that makes enterprise adoption possible later. Corporate communities are the inverse and tend to fail.

The documentation loop: transcripts → academic validation → community replication → wider adoption. Vancouver model → Surrey AI → regional chapters.

## The AI adoption curve (creative sector)

Creative professionals are 18–24 months behind enterprise in AI adoption but are the highest-value signal for consumer adoption. They adopt slowly because their identity is bound up in their craft, but when they adopt, they transform the tool's meaning.

Training creatives is not about feature education — it is about identity negotiation (am I still an artist if AI does X?).

## Indigenous economic models

$200B+ in Indigenous economic activity is currently invisible to standard economic measurement tools because those tools were designed to exclude it. Making it visible is a data engineering problem as much as a policy one.

Indigenomics model: sovereignty + self-determination + data sovereignty → economic legibility → resource flows.

## The "weird" advantage

Mainstream optimization converges on the same thing. The weird, the marginal, and the unexpected is where differentiation lives. This applies to communities, products, creative work, and careers.

Contrarian bets made 30 years early (Drupal in 2004, grassroots community in 2023) are only available to people who accept being misunderstood for extended periods.

## How to think about Athena / PAI

The PAI is not a chatbot — it is a context layer. The gap between what Athena can do and what it actually does is a context gap, not a capability gap. Every new telos file, every transcript ingested, every skill written closes that gap.

## How funding works in the BC nonprofit / tech intersection

Government grants (ETG, ISED, Heritage) are slow and process-heavy but predictable. Corporate sponsorship is fast and relationship-driven but lumpy. Earned revenue (training, services) is the stabilizer. The right mix varies by stage.
