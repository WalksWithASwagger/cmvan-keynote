# Mission

## Core Purpose

To humanize technology — to infuse it with empathy, ethics, and emotion — so that AI amplifies human creativity rather than replacing it.

## One-sentence version

I build bridges where human creativity and machine intelligence collide, creating weird, wonderful things neither could dream up alone.

## The longer version

I'm reprogramming the very core of our interactions with the digital world to resonate with the heartbeat of human experience.

I believe art shouldn't be a rarefied luxury but a common language for all. By democratizing access to AI and creative tools, I aim to unlock universal creative potential. Art is a right, not a privilege.

I don't build systems that optimize humans. I craft spaces where humans can be gloriously inefficient, creative, and alive.

The digital future belongs to those weird enough to imagine it differently.

## Why this matters

- Creative professionals are the most exposed and most under-supported group facing AI disruption.
- Indigenous communities need AI that reinforces sovereignty, not one that extracts and erases.
- BC's innovation ecosystem needs a grassroots counterweight to corporate AI narrative.
- Someone has to document all of this, obsessively, so future communities can learn from it.

## What I will not do

- Build systems that optimize humans for other humans' benefit.
- Treat AI as inevitable progress that people must accept.
- Prioritize growth metrics over community health.
- Let the weird, human, inefficient parts get engineered away.
