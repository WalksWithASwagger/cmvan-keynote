# Narratives

Stories KK tells about himself and his work — the frames that shape how he presents to the world.

## The core identity narrative

Techartist. Quasi-sage. Cyberpunk anti-hero from the future.

I build bridges where human creativity and machine intelligence collide, creating weird, wonderful things neither could dream up alone.

## The long arc narrative

I've been building websites and communities since 1995. I was 30 years ahead on open-source (Bryght/Drupal, 2005), 30 years ahead on open content (Dead.net, Grateful Dead, 2006), and I'm trying to be 30 years ahead on human-AI collaboration now.

The through-line is always the same: use technology to connect people, not replace them. Make the weird, human, creative parts thrive.

## The Vancouver AI narrative

What started as a grassroots meetup is now a living laboratory for what an inclusive, values-driven AI community looks like. We've put 3,000+ people through the door. Our model has been studied, documented, and replicated. Vancouver Magazine named us in the Power 50.

We did it without venture capital, without corporate capture, without losing the soul of the thing.

## The "building in public" narrative

I document everything — transcripts, videos, photos, academic papers, knowledge bases. Not for vanity. Because if we're going to figure out how to live well with AI, we need a record of what we tried. Future communities should be able to learn from us, not repeat our experiments from scratch.

## The Indigenomics narrative

Ancestral intelligence and digital futures are not opposites — they rhyme. Working alongside Carol Anne Hilton at Indigenomics Institute, we're making $200B+ in Indigenous economic activity legible to a world that was designed to ignore it. That's both a technical problem and a justice problem.

## The creative sector narrative

I'm not here to automate creatives out of a job. I'm here to help them become more themselves with these new tools. The photographers, writers, musicians, filmmakers — they're the canary in the coal mine for how AI reshapes identity and work. Helping them navigate that matters.

## The Upgrade AI narrative

Hundreds of creative professionals have trained with us. They didn't leave knowing more features — they left with a new relationship to their own practice. That's the transformation worth measuring.

## What I'm not

Not a tech bro. Not a corporate AI evangelist. Not neutral on whether AI is good.

The future belongs to those weird enough to imagine it differently.
