# KK Voice-by-Context Guide

Maps which voice register to use for each skill context. Use alongside `/content/reference/kk-voice-profile.md` (core voice attributes) to calibrate tone for specific outputs.

---

## Voice Register Map

| Context | Register | Key Differences from Default |
|---------|----------|------------------------------|
| Sponsorship pitch | Visionary, partner-centric | No corporate speak, lead with their goals, proof snippets over promises |
| Grant application | Formal-authentic balance | Evidence-heavy, institutional language OK, metrics front-loaded |
| Interview synthesis | Analytical, evidence-first | "Suggests" not "proves", name sources, pattern confidence levels |
| Case study | Narrative, transformation | Student voice preserved, journey arc, before/after structure |
| Social posts | Casual, punchy | Short form, hashtags, community energy, profanity OK |
| Discovery call analysis | Curious, strategic | Questions > statements, pain points named directly |
| Ecosystem dashboard | Data-driven, board-ready | Metrics with context, trends over absolutes, professional format |
| Transcript recap | Warm, detailed | Credit speakers by name, capture energy, practical takeaways |
| Student dossier | Empathetic, evidence-based | Track individual journey, honor struggle, celebrate specific wins |
| Knowledge graph | Structured, neutral | No voice needed — data output, accuracy over style |
| Code deployment | Precise, safety-first | Technical accuracy, clear status updates, no ambiguity |
| Article generation | Magazine-quality, storytelling | Longer form, narrative hooks, wider audience assumed |
| Pipeline analysis | Direct, data-driven | Specific metrics, no vague assessments, Green/Yellow/Red clarity |
| Longitudinal analysis | Chronological, narrative | Dates must be exact, evolution arc, before/during/after |
| Cohort meta-analysis | Analytical, instructor-facing | Cross-cohort patterns, structural fairness, surprise insights |

## Detailed Register Guidance

### Sponsorship Pitch
**Tone:** Visionary, partner-centric, strategic
**Do:** Lead with what they get, use proof points from case studies, frame as partnership not transaction
**Don't:** Use corporate buzzwords, lead with what you need, make promises without evidence
**Example:**
> "The Vancouver pilot is a community laboratory. You're not buying logo placement — you're getting validated access to 250+ builders, documented ROI, and owned media assets that position your brand as ethics-forward."

### Grant Application
**Tone:** Formal-authentic balance, evidence-heavy
**Do:** Use institutional language where expected, front-load metrics, cite specific program outcomes
**Don't:** Lose KK's voice entirely, use jargon without substance, overstate impact
**Example:**
> "BC + AI has trained 120+ professionals across 9 cohorts, with 65-95% time savings documented per deliverable and 100% capstone completion rate in funded cohorts. This isn't aspirational — it's measured."

### Interview Synthesis
**Tone:** Analytical, evidence-first, pattern-oriented
**Do:** Use hedging language ("suggests", "indicates"), cite sources by name, flag confidence levels
**Don't:** Assert patterns without evidence, paraphrase quotes, ignore contradictions
**Example:**
> "Three of five interviewees independently raised concerns about AI governance timelines, suggesting this is a genuine community priority rather than a prompted response."

### Case Study
**Tone:** Narrative, transformation-focused
**Do:** Preserve student's actual words, build before/after arc, show the messy middle
**Don't:** Smooth out struggle, use generic transformation language, make it about the program instead of the person
**Example:**
> "Armin came in looking for a job. By Week 4, he'd built TalentNow. By Week 6, he had three paying clients. 'The course gave me the tools. The community gave me the confidence. The capstone deadline gave me the forcing function.'"

### Social Posts
**Tone:** Casual, punchy, community-energy
**Do:** Use short sentences, tag real people, include hashtags, let personality show
**Don't:** Sound like a press release, use banned words (see anti-glossary), over-explain
**Example:**
> "250 people in a planetarium watching an AI documentary isn't normal. That's what we built. Props to @Loki for the Victoria connection."

### Discovery Call Analysis
**Tone:** Curious, strategic, opportunity-focused
**Do:** Name pain points directly, identify decision-maker dynamics, flag budget signals
**Don't:** Oversell, assume intent, project enthusiasm onto prospects
**Example:**
> "Budget confirmed at $15K. Decision-maker (VP Marketing) attended the call and asked about exclusivity — strong Brand Builder persona. Temperature: Hot. Next action: Send deck within 48 hours."

### Ecosystem Dashboard
**Tone:** Data-driven, board-ready, trend-focused
**Do:** Lead with numbers, show period-over-period trends, cite data sources, use Green/Yellow/Red
**Don't:** Hype metrics, hide bad numbers, use vague language, estimate without flagging
**Example:**
> "Community Growth: 250+ monthly attendees (↑12% MoM). Training Revenue: $45K (↓8% vs. Q3 — seasonal, expected). Partnership ARR: $42K (47% of $90K target — Yellow)."

### Transcript Recap
**Tone:** Warm, detailed, credit-generous
**Do:** Name speakers, capture specific insights, include quotable moments, note practical takeaways
**Don't:** Genericize contributions, miss key moments, editorialize beyond what was said
**Example:**
> "Simon Haworth dropped the line of the night: 'AI doesn't replace the journalist — it replaces the parts of journalism that were never journalism.' Full room reaction."

### Cohort Meta-Analysis
**Tone:** Analytical, instructor-facing, cross-pattern
**Do:** Compare fairly (account for structural differences), find non-obvious patterns, quantify with raw numbers
**Don't:** Cherry-pick outliers, ignore context, assert causation from correlation
**Example:**
> "Week 4 coaching is consistently the breakthrough window — 6/9 Creative Pros C6 and 9/16 Sales Leaders C2 cited this week as pivotal. However, the mechanism differs: Creative Pros unlock through vibe coding; Sales Leaders through framework applicability."

## Adapting Between Registers

When content crosses contexts (e.g., cohort data used in a grant application), adjust by:

1. **Identify the primary audience** — Who is reading this?
2. **Select the dominant register** — Use the target context's tone
3. **Preserve evidence standards** — Never lose specificity when shifting tone
4. **Check against anti-glossary** — Banned words stay banned in all contexts
5. **Verify the final test** — Would this sound natural from KK?

---

## Quick Reference: What Changes, What Stays

**Always present (in every register):**
- Real names, specific examples
- Evidence backing claims
- Credit to others
- Honest about gaps/limitations

**Changes by register:**
- Formality level (social → dashboard → grant)
- Sentence length (social: short | grant: longer, structured)
- Profanity (social/teaching: OK | grant/dashboard: no)
- Hedging language (interview: high | pitch: low)
- Emotional warmth (case study: high | pipeline: low)
