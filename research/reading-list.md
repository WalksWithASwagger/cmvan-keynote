# Research & Reading List: Punk Rock AI

An annotated bibliography of the core ideas, cultural touchpoints, and technical theories informing the "Punk Rock AI" Creative Mornings Vancouver 2026 Keynote.

## 1. The Heritage of Sampling & Appropriation
*   **"Cut-Up Method" by William S. Burroughs and Brion Gysin (1959):** The original concept of cutting printed paper and rearranging it into literature, showing that selection and rearrangement are the core of creativity.
*   **"The Society of the Spectacle" by Guy Debord (1967):** The foundational text for the Situationists; introduced *détournement*, the hijacking of capitalist or corporate messaging to subvert its meaning.
*   **"Sniffin' Glue" Zine History (Mark Perry, 1976):** Documenting how access to corporate Xerox machines allowed the punk aesthetic and community to bypass traditional gatekeepers entirely.

## 2. Digital Identity & The "Selector"
*   **"Can’t Stop Won’t Stop: A History of the Hip-Hop Generation" by Jeff Chang:** Essential reading on how Jamaican "selectors" (Count Machuki, Kool Herc) brought the concept of taste-as-a-skill to the Bronx, accidentally inventing Hip Hop with two turntables.

## 3. Generative AI as The New Xerox
*   **"Your Taste is Your Moat" (Kris Krüg, KB Article):** Articulates the idea that AI made generation cheap, so judgment and selection are the only things keeping creatives relevant.
*   **"Both Hands Full" (Kris Krüg, KB Article):** The philosophical stance of maintaining deep critique of AI's theft while simultaneously embracing its unprecedented capabilities.

## 4. Vancouver Cultural Context
*   **Adbusters / Culture Jamming:** The legacy of turning the master's tools against the master in Vancouver; the origins of Buy Nothing Day and Occupy Wall Street.
*   **"RIP: A Remix Manifesto" by Brett Gaylor (2008):** Highlighting Canada's (and Vancouver's) role in the open-source and anti-copyright cultural movement.
