# Research Dossier & Story Bank: Punk Rock AI

## The Avant-Garde & Punk Lineage (Historical Context)
- **1916 (Dadaism):** Dada poets in Zurich cutting up newspapers and rearranging them on the floor to form new poems. This was sampling decades before vinyl existed.
- **William S. Burroughs:** Used the "cut-up technique" with scissors on existing texts and claimed it as literature. He proved that arrangement and selection *is* the medium.
- **The Situationists:** French avant-garde who coined *détournement* (turning the tools of the spectacle against the spectacle). Using capitalist infrastructure to subvert it.
- **1976 (The Xerox Machine):** A London bank clerk named Mark Perry saw the Ramones, went home, and used a defense contractor's Xerox machine to print sixty copies of *Sniffin' Glue*. Punk rock finally had a press.
- **1976 (The Jamaican Selector):** In Kingston, the person picking the records at the sound system dance was called the "selector." The skill wasn't playing the instrument—it was knowing what track the room needed. Taste was the scarcity. Count Machuki chewed matchsticks, toasted over records, and accidentally invented rap.
- **1520 Sedgwick Ave:** Clive Campbell (Kool Herc) takes the Kingston selector concept to the Bronx. Hip-hop is born.  
- **Sears Catalog ($49 Silvertone):** Took the electric guitar out of the hands of the elite and put it in the bedrooms of teenagers. Garage rock explodes.
- **Indymedia (1999):** Activists hacked together open-source publishing tools to out-traffic CNN during the Seattle WTO protests.

**The Historical Pattern:** Corporations build the infrastructure. Weirdos, kids, and artists figure out what it's *actually* for.

## Personal & Vancouver Context (The Story Bank)
- **Kris's 1,800 Photos:** AI companies scraped Flickr and Creative Commons datasets. Over 1,800 of Kris's photos were sucked into the models without permission or consent. It is theft. It is non-consensual. 
- **The True Contradiction:** Even operating post-theft, Kris is more creative, productive, and powerful than ever before. This is the Punk Condition: *Holding critique in one hand, and capability in the other.*
- **Vancouver as the Hub:** Vancouver isn't a sleepy coastal town; it's the home of Greenpeace and Adbusters. It is ground zero for taking corporate megastructures and turning them into counterculture.

## Creator Case Studies (To Use on Stage)
- **Matlock (27 Years):** Sat on cassette tapes for almost three decades because the friction of finishing was too high. AI gave him the final 15% to push his album *R3ALMS* across the line.
- **Luke Minaker:** 30 years building physical monsters. The ratio was always 80% drudgery, 20% creative joy. AI flipped the ratio. "I don't care about the money. I am so excited that I get to do art again."
- **Belgium (Actress):** Bedridden for 18 months due to illness. "AI film is my hope to be able to continue to exist and to tell something." Before we get abstract about ethics, we must look at what AI means for accessibility.
- **Kevin Friel:** 25-year Hollywood VFX veteran. AI isn't the orchestra; it gives the conductor a bigger orchestra. "Let's use these tools to f*** it up y'all."
- **Vicki (Visual Artist):** Uploaded 20 of her analog photos and asked AI to abstract the patterns. Realized her intuition was just deep pattern recognition. Taste can be articulated.

## The Philosophical Framework
- **Fair vs. Frequent:** AI knows what's frequent on the internet. It does not know what is fair. Stop using the word "bias." Start using words like theft, racism, and misogyny. The mirror reflects; we correct.
- **Taste as the Moat:** Now that AI can generate a poem or an image in 3 seconds, generation is a cheap commodity. The only thing left that is scarce is *selection*. Your taste.
