## The thesis

Every cultural shift that mattered started the same way: someone picks up a tool built by a massive corporation and uses it wrong.

- Dadaists cut up newspapers in 1916 — sampling before vinyl
- Burroughs did it with scissors
- A bank clerk Xeroxed sixty copies of *Sniffin' Glue* on a defense contractor's machine and punk had a press
- Selectors in Kingston picked records at the sound system because taste was the skill — that boat ride to the Bronx became hip-hop

AI is the next Xerox machine. The pattern is the same.

**The Punk Condition:** critique in one hand, capability in the other. If every thoughtful, ethical, critical creative opts out, the people who don't see the problems write the rules.

**The framework (3 moves):**
1. **Name what you're seeing.** Stop saying "bias." Say theft. Say racism. Say misogyny. AI knows what's frequent, not what's fair.
2. **Externalize your taste.** Three documents in ten minutes — Personal Policy + Style Guide + Worldview — and the machine stops giving you average.
3. **Ship something by Release Day.** Punk rock deadline. Not perfect. Punk never was.
